Mysuru

Known as the cultural capital of Karnataka, Mysuru has transitioned from its royal heritage and silk industry prominence to becoming a thriving center for education, IT services, and tourism. The city's economy now thrives on software technology parks, educational institutions, and a growing tourism sector, making it a vibrant blend of tradition and modernity.

Chamundi Hill

Chamundi Hill, or Chamundi Betta, is a revered landmark situated about 13 kilometers from Mysuru in Karnataka, India. Rising to around 1,000 meters, it is known for the Chamundeshwari Temple, dedicated to the fierce goddess Chamundeshwari, believed to protect the city. The hill also holds historical and cultural significance, with a giant monolithic Nandi statue and Mahishasura statue symbolizing the legendary tale of the goddess defeating the demon Mahishasura. Chamundi Hill offers a panoramic view of Mysuru, attracting both devotees and tourists to its scenic heights and spiritual atmosphere.


Mysuru Palace

Mysuru Palace, located in the heart of Mysuru, is a renowned historical site and former royal residence dedicated to the Wadiyar dynasty of Mysore. Known for its stunning Indo-Saracenic architecture, the palace showcases a unique blend of Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic styles, making it a cultural treasure and symbol of Karnataka's royal heritage.


St. Philomena's Cathedral

St. Philomena's Church, located in Mysuru, is a famous landmark dedicated to Saint Philomena, reflecting the city’s rich architectural heritage. Built in a neo-Gothic style, the church is known for its tall twin spires and beautiful stained-glass windows that depict scenes from the life of Christ. It stands as one of the tallest churches in Asia and a significant symbol of Mysuru's cultural diversity.


Karanji Lake

Karanji Lake, located in Mysuru, is a popular recreational spot surrounded by lush greenery and rich biodiversity. Known for its serene atmosphere, the lake features a large aviary and butterfly park, making it an ideal location for nature lovers and birdwatchers. It serves as a peaceful escape within the city and is a cherished part of Mysuru’s natural landscape.


Jaganmohan palace

Jaganmohan Palace, located in Mysuru, is a historic palace known for its beautiful architecture and cultural significance. Originally used as a royal residence by the Wadiyar family, it now houses an art gallery featuring an extensive collection of paintings, sculptures, and artifacts, including works by Raja Ravi Varma. Jaganmohan Palace stands as a symbol of Mysuru's rich artistic and royal heritage.


Bonsai Garden

The Bonsai Garden, located in Mysuru within the premises of the Avadhoota Datta Peetham, is a unique attraction showcasing a wide variety of meticulously cultivated bonsai trees. With over 100 species on display, the garden offers a peaceful environment for visitors to appreciate the art of miniature tree cultivation. It stands as a serene spot that reflects Mysuru's dedication to preserving natural beauty and horticultural artistry.


Railway Museum

The Railway Museum in Mysuru is a fascinating destination dedicated to the rich history of Indian Railways. It features a collection of vintage locomotives, carriages, and artifacts that showcase the evolution of rail transport in India. Visitors can explore the museum's outdoor exhibits and learn about the significance of railways in shaping the country's infrastructure. The Railway Museum serves as an engaging educational experience and a tribute to the heritage of rail travel in Mysuru.


Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens (Mysuru Zoo)

The Mysuru Zoo, officially known as Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens, is one of the oldest and most well-maintained zoos in India. Located near the city center, it is home to a diverse range of animals, including rare and endangered species. The zoo emphasizes conservation and education, providing visitors with a chance to learn about wildlife in a lush, natural setting. It is a popular family destination and an essential part of Mysuru's cultural and ecological landscape.


Lalitha Mahal

The Lalitha Mahal Palace, located on the outskirts of Mysuru, is an exquisite palace originally built for the royal family as a guesthouse for the Viceroy of India. Known for its stunning architecture blending Indo-Saracenic and European styles, the palace features luxurious interiors and sprawling gardens. Today, it serves as a heritage hotel, allowing visitors to experience the grandeur of Mysuru's royal history while enjoying breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.


Kaarya Siddhi Anjaneya Swami Temple

The Kaarya Siddhi Anjaneya Swami Temple, situated in Mysuru, is a revered spiritual site dedicated to Lord Hanuman. Known for its serene ambiance, the temple attracts devotees seeking blessings for the fulfillment of their wishes and aspirations. The temple's beautiful architecture and tranquil surroundings make it a popular destination for both worshippers and tourists looking for a peaceful retreat.


Kukkarahalli Lake

Kukkarahalli Lake is a beautiful, serene lake located in the heart of Mysuru. Popular with both locals and tourists, it offers a peaceful environment for walking, jogging, and birdwatching. The lake is surrounded by a lush green landscape and attracts various bird species, making it an ideal spot for nature enthusiasts. It has a well-maintained walking path around it, giving visitors picturesque views of the water and surrounding greenery, especially at sunrise and sunset.


ISKCON

ISKCON Mysuru is a renowned spiritual and cultural center dedicated to Lord Krishna. It’s known for its stunning temple architecture, vibrant festivals, and peaceful ambiance. The temple hosts daily prayers, cultural events, and spiritual discourses, drawing visitors and devotees from near and far. ISKCON Mysuru also has beautiful surroundings that offer a calming retreat from the busy city, making it a popular spot for those looking for spiritual enrichment and tranquility.


Krishna Raja Sagara

KRS Dam, built across the Kaveri River, is an engineering marvel constructed in 1932. Located near Mysuru, it supplies water for irrigation and drinking purposes in the region. Adjacent to the dam is the Brindavan Gardens, famous for its musical fountains and beautifully manicured lawns. The dam and gardens together create a scenic and popular tourist spot, especially during evenings when the fountains are illuminated.


GRS Fantasy Park

GRS Fantasy Park is an amusement and water park located on the outskirts of Mysuru. It offers various rides and water slides, including wave pools, lazy rivers, and thrilling water games, making it ideal for families and thrill-seekers. It’s a perfect destination for a day out, with a blend of adventure and relaxation.


Venugopala Swamy Temple

Located near the KRS Dam, Venugopalaswamy Temple is an architectural marvel dedicated to Lord Krishna. The temple is a replica of an ancient temple that was submerged due to the creation of the KRS Dam reservoir. Reconstructed with intricate Hoysala-style carvings, this peaceful temple by the water offers a serene ambiance and beautiful views, especially at sunset.


Payana Car Museum

The Payana Car Museum in Mysuru showcases a diverse collection of vintage and classic cars. It’s an exciting place for automobile enthusiasts, with exhibits ranging from early 20th-century models to more recent iconic cars. Each vehicle is well-maintained and provides a glimpse into the evolution of automotive design and technology.


Chunchanakatte Falls

Chunchanakatte Falls is located on the Kaveri River near Mysuru. Surrounded by lush greenery, the waterfall cascades down in two segments, creating a mesmerizing view. Close to the falls is a temple dedicated to Lord Kodanda Rama, adding a spiritual element to the natural beauty of the location


Brindavan Gardens

Located below the KRS Dam, Brindavan Gardens is a sprawling, terraced garden known for its symmetrically designed flowerbeds, ornamental plants, and musical fountains. The evening light and sound show at the fountains is a major attraction. The garden’s stunning landscaping and vibrant atmosphere make it a must-visit spot in Mysuru.


Balmuri Falls

Balmuri Falls is a man-made waterfall created by a check dam on the Kaveri River. It’s a popular picnic spot with shallow waters, making it safe for wading and bathing. The location’s natural beauty and relaxing atmosphere attract families and groups looking for a scenic getaway close to Mysuru.


Kabini dam

The Kabini Dam, located near Mysuru, is built across the Kabini River. It serves as an important source of water for agriculture and drinking in the region. The surrounding Kabini backwaters are famous for their scenic beauty and are a haven for wildlife, attracting photographers, birdwatchers, and nature lovers.


Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary, near Mysuru, is the largest bird sanctuary in Karnataka and a paradise for birdwatchers. Situated along the banks of the Kaveri River, it’s home to a wide variety of native and migratory bird species. Visitors can enjoy boat rides, which offer close-up views of birds and sometimes even crocodiles in their natural habitat.


Mysore Sand Sculpture Museum

The Sand Museum in Mysuru features intricate sand sculptures depicting themes from history, mythology, wildlife, and culture. It is one of the unique attractions in Mysuru, showcasing the skill of local artists. The sculptures are created entirely from sand, making it a popular spot for art and culture enthusiasts.


Shukavana

Shukavana is an aviary and bird rehabilitation center founded by Sri Ganapathy Sachchidananda Swamiji in Mysuru. It is home to a wide variety of exotic birds from around the world, making it an educational experience for bird lovers. Shukavana has been recognized for its diverse species, and visitors can enjoy the close-up view of colorful parrots, macaws, and other birds.

Icons of Mysuru

Krishna Raja Wadiyar IV

Krishnaraja Wadiyar IV (1884–1940) was the 24th Maharaja of Mysore, born in Mysuru. He modernized the state, founded the University of Mysore, and supported arts and education.

Sir M Visvesvaraya

Sir M. Visvesvaraya (1861–1962) was an engineer and statesman, born in Muddenahalli, Karnataka. He is known for building the Krishnaraja Sagar Dam and modernizing Mysore.
